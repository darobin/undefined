The faculty search posting requested someone «deeply versed in a minimum of two fields ». In my case, it is very precisely two: «Web technology» taken in a broad sense that reaches across numerous domains, and «Miscellaneous». I am particularly good at the latter.

The reason I must apply for this position is simple: I am spoiling to help bring about a better world, but my interests are more eclectic than can easily be brought to fruition within most existing structures. I am trying to implement this multifarious drive as a freelance tinkering philosopher, but progress has been slow. Many of my ideas and areas of interest are having to lay fallow as I build opportunities to tend to them step by step. Suffice it to say that finding out the Media Lab was opening just such a position, with no academic requirements, was electrifying.

I include here a short selection of ideas. I make no claim that they are necessarily right, but I do believe that they have to potential to be usefully wrong. With some rigorous curiosity and weather-hardened practice some could take a twisted and hopefully troublesome path to improving the world.
